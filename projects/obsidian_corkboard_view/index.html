Overview

What it does

Corkboard View brings the Scrivener corkboard experience directly into Obsidian. Rather than looking at a flat list of file names, you get a visual overview of your notes as index cards on a cork-textured board — each card showing the note's title, a content excerpt, word count, and how recently it was edited.

The plugin is designed to stay out of your way. It automatically tracks whichever folder you're working in: open a note, and the corkboard updates to show all notes from that folder with a smooth fade transition. You can also manually override this to view any folder — or your entire vault — whenever you need the wider view.

Cards can be reordered by dragging, and right-clicking a card gives you options to open, open in a new tab, or delete. For customisation, you can swap the cork texture for any image via the plugin settings.

Features
🗂️
Index Cards
Notes rendered as clean editorial cards with title, excerpt, word count, and last-modified time
📁
Auto-Follow Folders
Corkboard automatically updates to show the folder of your currently active note
✋
Drag to Reorder
Drag cards to rearrange their order on the board
🌅
Custom Backgrounds
Replace the default cork texture with any image URL via Settings → Corkboard View
🌙
Dark Mode
Fully styled for both Obsidian light and dark themes
✍️
Editorial Typography
Crimson Pro and Newsreader serif fonts give it a literary, writer-friendly feel
Installation
1
Download the .zip above and extract it. You need main.js, manifest.json, and styles.css.
2
In your Obsidian vault, navigate to .obsidian/plugins/ and create a new folder called corkboard-view.
3
Copy main.js, manifest.json, and styles.css into that folder.
4
Restart Obsidian, then go to Settings → Community Plugins and enable Corkboard View.
5
Click the grid icon in the left sidebar, or open the command palette and search "Open Corkboard".
